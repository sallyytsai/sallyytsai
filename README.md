pressure — with Taiwan as my primary case and causal inference as my primary tool.
Current work: security salience and trade restrictions (cross-national, Taiwan as the
crucial case); VAT pass-through in Philippine pharmaceutical markets; SME supply chain
adjustment under geopolitical disruption. Native Mandarin enables direct access to
MOEA/SMEA firm data and Legislative Yuan records.

### Working Papers

**[The Security Premium: Why Taiwan Maintains the World's Most Restrictive Policy toward Chinese Electric Vehicles](https://sallyytsai.github.io/sally-portfolio/pdfs/tsai-wang-sanchez-security-premium-2026.pdf)**
*w/ Wang & Sanchez · HKS DPI-410 · Spring 2026*

Cross-national ordered logit (N≈60) + synthetic control + process trace. Taiwan is the
largest positive residual in the industry-only model — security salience, not security
threat level, moves policy. Leave-one-out cross-validation gives Taiwan only 4%
probability of maximum restriction absent the security channel.

**[VAT Pass-Through in Philippine Pharmaceutical Markets: Evidence from the TRAIN Act](https://sallyytsai.github.io/sally-portfolio/pdfs/tsai-vega-vat-passthrough-2026.pdf)**
*w/ Vega (Philippine Competition Commission / University of East Anglia) · 2026*

DiD + triple-difference by license type + synthetic control on IQVIA MIDAS data
(N=44,339). Zero pass-through to patients — firms absorbed the 2019 VAT exemption as
margin expansion; retail volumes fell 20%; double welfare loss in the retail channel.
